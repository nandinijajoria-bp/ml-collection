Rate of annualized penal charges (if any)

Example: Borrower has taken a loan of Rs 1,00,000 for 12 months at 1.59% interest rate; EDI will be Rs 331 and effective APR will be 47.04%

If the borrower does not pay EDI continuously for 7 days, a sum of Rs 2,317 (331*7) is overdue from the customer. As per the penal charges grid, late payment charges of Rs 250 (inclusive of GST) will get levied to the borrower as overdue amount is between Rs 1,000 - Rs 5,000. The next penalty can only be levied after 30 days if borrower at that time has 7 or more EDIs overdue.

Case 1: If borrower paid only one penalty charge during its loan tenure, effective APR for the loan will be 47.58%

Case 2: If borrower paid two penalty charges during its loan tenure, effective APR for the loan will be 47.93%
